The Clever Fox and the Lion

Once upon a time, in a large forest, there lived a strong lion. All the animals were afraid of him because he hunted many animals every day. The animals decided to make a plan to save themselves. They went to the lion and requested that only one animal would come to him daily as food. The lion agreed.

One day, it was the turn of a clever fox. The fox did not want to die, so he thought of an idea. He slowly walked toward the lion’s cave and reached very late. The angry lion asked why he was late.

The fox replied that another lion in the forest had stopped him and claimed to be the real king. Hearing this, the lion became furious and asked the fox to take him to the other lion.

The clever fox took the lion to a deep well filled with water. The lion looked inside the well and saw his own reflection. Thinking it was another lion, he jumped into the well to fight. He fell into the water and drowned.

The animals of the forest became very happy because the clever fox had saved everyone with his intelligence.

Moral: Intelligence is more powerful than strength.
